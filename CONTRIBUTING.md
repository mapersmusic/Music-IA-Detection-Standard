# Contributing to Music-AI-Detection-Standard

First off, thank you for considering contributing to this standard! Your expertise and observations are what will keep this framework relevant as AI technology evolves.

## How You Can Help

There are several ways to contribute to the standard:

### 1. Proposing New Criteria
If you notice a new recurring pattern or a specific "red flag" in AI-generated music accounts (behavioral, metadata-based, or technical), please:
* **Open an Issue**: Describe the pattern you've observed with anonymized examples if possible.
* **Submit a Pull Request**: If you have a clear idea of the weight (High/Medium) and description, add it directly to the `CRITERIA.md` file.

### 2. Refining Thresholds
Our current thresholds (e.g., >50 tracks per year) are based on initial observations. If you have data or case studies suggesting these should be adjusted to be more accurate or to reduce false positives, please share your findings.

### 3. Reporting Edge Cases
If you find a human artist who triggers several "AI Probable" signals, or an AI account that bypasses current criteria, let us know. These edge cases help us refine the "Protective Factors."

## Guidelines for Contributions

To maintain the integrity and focus of this repository, please keep the following principles in mind:

* **Behavioral Focus**: We prioritize criteria based on "common sense," intent, and verifiable metadata over complex audio signal processing.
* **Objectivity**: Criteria should be based on facts that can be checked (release dates, credits, public presence) rather than subjective musical taste.
* **Clarity**: When proposing a new criterion, explain *why* it is a signal of AI generation and *how* a moderator can verify it.

## Process

1. **Fork** the repository.
2. **Create a new branch** for your suggestion.
3. **Submit a Pull Request** with a clear description of the changes.
4. The community and maintainers will review the proposal for consistency with the "Common Sense" philosophy.
